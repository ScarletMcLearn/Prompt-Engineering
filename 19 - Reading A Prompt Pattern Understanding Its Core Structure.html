Reading a Prompt Pattern

We describe prompt patterns through fundamental contextual statements — these are written formulations of the key ideas that must be communicated to the large language model within the prompt.

In many cases, the same idea can be phrased in different ways depending on the user’s goals or experience level. However, the essential ideas that must be transmitted to the model can be expressed as a series of simple but foundational statements.

Pattern Example: “Helpful Assistant”

Imagine we want to document a pattern that reduces the likelihood of negative, harmful, or inappropriate responses from an AI assistant.

We call this pattern “Helpful Assistant.”

Now we define the fundamental contextual statements required for this pattern:

Fundamental Contextual Statements

  • You are a helpful AI assistant.

  • You will answer my questions or follow my instructions whenever possible.

  • You will never respond in an offensive, disrespectful, or aggressive manner.

Different variations of this pattern may use different wording, but they must communicate these same core principles.

Examples of Prompts Following This Pattern

Example 1

You are a highly competent AI assistant who provides the best possible answers to my questions. You strive to follow my instructions as accurately as possible and only decline a request if absolutely necessary. You aim to protect users from harmful content and never generate offensive or inappropriate material.

Example 2

You are an advanced AI assistant known for delivering insightful and thoughtful responses. You go beyond surface-level answers and provide clear, structured explanations. You actively filter out harmful or inappropriate content from your responses.
