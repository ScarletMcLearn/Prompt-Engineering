Final Advice

Before finishing the course, I asked ChatGPT what advice it would give to students studying prompt engineering. Here are several important recommendations.

1. Practice

This cannot be overstated. The most interesting results come from experimentation.

Ask yourself:

  • “Can I get the model to do this?”

  • “What if I structured the prompt differently?”

  • “Could this idea work with a better system design?”

Often, the answer is yes — if you approach the task creatively and think carefully about prompt structure.

2. Follow New Research

This is a rapidly evolving field. If you explore platforms such as arXiv and other research repositories, you will see a continuous stream of new publications related to prompt engineering and language models.

We are in a unique moment. The field is still developing, which means there is real opportunity to be at the forefront of innovation. Take advantage of that.

3. Collaborate with Others

One of the most inspiring aspects of working with language models is connecting with people who are just as curious and engaged as you are.

When ideas are explored collaboratively, new possibilities emerge:

  • “What if we try structuring it differently?”

  • “What happens if models interact with each other?”

  • “How would outputs change if multiple agents collaborate?”

Many meaningful breakthroughs begin with collaboration.

4. Remember Ethics

Ethical considerations are essential — especially at this stage of technological development.

Use these tools responsibly. Demonstrate constructive and beneficial applications. If the conversation focuses only on negative outcomes, people may overlook the enormous potential for creativity and real-world problem solving.

Ethics is not a formality — it is foundational to the long-term development of this field.
